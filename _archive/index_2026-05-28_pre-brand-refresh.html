80 Years in the Making

Why Your Sauce
Will Taste Like It Should.

In 1945, Ernest Ricci opened a small butcher shop in McKees Rocks, Pennsylvania. He brought with him a spice blend from his family's hometown of Sulmona, Italy — and a stubborn refusal to change it.

Eight decades later, not a single ingredient has been altered. The same hand-mixed spices — real paprika, whole fennel seed, no ground shortcuts. The same proprietary grind. Small batches, all natural, no additives, no preservatives. The same sausage that made Ricci's a Pittsburgh institution.

Today, three generations of master sausage-makers still run the counter — and for the first time in our history, we're shipping to doorsteps across the country.
