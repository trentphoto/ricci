I.
Origini

A spice blend carried from Sulmona.

Sulmona sits on a ridge in the Abruzzo mountains, a small Italian city known for confetti — sugared almonds — and for cured meats that have been cut the same way since long before the Ricci family kept shop there.

The spices were mixed at home. Nobody wrote them down. When Ernest Ricci crossed the Atlantic, the blend came with him in his head — and every link of sausage we make today traces back to it, unchanged.

"The recipe hasn't changed. Not once. Not a single ingredient."

That is the entire premise of this shop. No line extensions. No modernization. The same hands, the same hand-mixed blend, the same proprietary grind since 1945.

II.
Il Metodo

From cut to counter.

Four steps. One room. The same sequence every morning since 1945. This is how a link of Ricci's is made — and why it has never been imitated.

Passaggio I

The Blend

The Sulmona family blend. Measured by hand, in the same order, every time. Nothing from a bag.

Passaggio II

The Grind

A proprietary cut — coarser than most — that holds the spice and keeps the juice. The same dies we've used for decades.

Passaggio III

The Link

Hand-linked into rope. Every sausage in the case was tied by a person who knows what a good link feels like.

Passaggio IV

The Rest

Cold rest before the case, before the pan, before the plate. Patience is part of the recipe — and it can't be hurried.
