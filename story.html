Our Story

Since 1945.

Eighty years of one recipe. One family. One city.
And now — the whole country.

Where It Began

The recipe didn't start in Pittsburgh.

It started in Sulmona, a small hilltop city in the Abruzzo region of Italy — known for centuries as the home of confetti (sugared almonds) and hand-crafted cured meats. The Ricci family kept a spice blend there, passed down through generations, that nobody outside the family ever wrote down.

When Ernest Ricci came to America, he brought it with him.

"The recipe hasn't changed. Not once. Not a single ingredient."

1945: McKees Rocks, Pennsylvania

Ernest opened a small butcher shop at 500 Pine Hollow Road in McKees Rocks — a working-class Pittsburgh borough with a dense Italian-American community that knew good sausage the moment they tasted it.

Word spread fast. Families made the drive from across Pittsburgh. Restaurants called. By the time the Korean War ended, Ricci's Italian Sausage was already becoming something rare in American food: a true original, with no competition, no imitators, and a customer base that came back every single week.

In 1973, the USDA showed up. From that day on, an inspector has walked our floor every morning we've been open — which is how we've been able to send our sausage to people outside Pittsburgh ever since.

2026

A New Era Begins

Ricci's enters a new chapter. The recipe stays the same. The mission gets bigger: bringing Pittsburgh's most beloved Italian sausage to doorsteps across America.

The Recipe That Hasn't Changed

We're not going to tell you what's in it.

What we can tell you is this: the spice blend is mixed by hand, in the same sequence, every single time. The grind is proprietary. The process is the same as it was in 1945. There are no shortcuts because there is no shortcut that produces this flavor.

When the holidays come and we can't keep up, we put a limit on how much any one customer can buy. Not because we're precious about it — because we'd rather send a slightly smaller order to everyone than cut corners to make more.

Every week, the line forms before we open.

Now — Shipping to You

For eighty years, the only way to get Ricci's was to drive to McKees Rocks. That's starting to change.

We're now packing sausage on dry ice and sending it to families across the country — Pittsburgh transplants, folks who tasted it at a friend's Sunday dinner, people who've heard about it for years and finally want to try it themselves.

The recipe hasn't changed. The team hasn't changed. Only the address of the people we get to feed.

What Stays the Same

Everything that matters. The recipe. The facility. The team — some of whom have been making sausage at 500 Pine Hollow Road for over a decade. The daily USDA inspection. The hand-mixed spice blend. The refusal to compromise on flavor for the sake of convenience or cost.

The store in McKees Rocks is still open. The same six days a week schedule. The same team behind the counter. The same faces many Pittsburgh families have seen every week for years. That doesn't change.
