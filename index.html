80 Years in the Making

A Recipe Worth
Keeping Secret.

In 1945, Ernest Ricci opened a small butcher shop in McKees Rocks, Pennsylvania. He brought with him a spice blend from his family's hometown of Sulmona, Italy — and a stubborn refusal to change it.

Eight decades later, not a single ingredient has been altered. The same hand-mixed spices. The same proprietary grind. The same sausage that made Ricci's a Pittsburgh institution.

Now, Ricci's is entering a new era — with a fresh mission: to bring the most authentic Italian sausage in America to doorsteps across the country.

Why USDA Federal Inspection Matters

Ricci's has held USDA Federal Inspection since 1973 — one of the rarest certifications in American food manufacturing. It means daily on-site inspection, the highest food safety standards, and the legal ability to ship fresh sausage to all 50 states. Most regional producers don't have it.
